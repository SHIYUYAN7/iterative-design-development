Project Introduction

In this project, we designed an interactive prototype for Allara’s website based on the problems they face on their original website. Allara is a company that provides virtual care for women’s hormonal and metabolic health.

Prototype Goal

Based on the project brief provided by our client, we communicated with our client to clarify our understanding of the target customers, problems faced on their original website, and some ideas on the complexity of the final design. The revised project brief was created with the additional insights obtained.

Problem statement:

  • Patients are not sure if Allara is the right fit for them
  • Patients do not know who Allara is
  • Patients are skeptical of doctors

Low-fi Wireframe

For our first low-fidelity wireframe, we chose designs from our sketches that would best solve the problems the client had with the site. More specifically, we addressed the problem of users potentially being unfamiliar with Allara, uncertain if it suits their needs, and being skeptical about the doctors by changing the flow of the content, and adding narratives as well as interactive elements. In addition, we tried to make choices that would allow personalized and inclusive experiences for the users.

Visual Style Guide

For the design of color, font and size, we chose and kept it consistent with the original design to the greatest extent, including all of the text sizes, the effect of mouse hover, the color after being selected, and the original color of the button, etc. For the customer review cards, we also added a three-dot icon for the sliding window and made it the same color as the theme color so that it can blend in with the page.

At the same time, we also made some changes to the original design. For example, on the input box, we added a shadow to the selected and hover input box. In this way, users who are not sensitive to color can clearly see the input box they selected.

In the design of the icon, we implemented three blue buttons to increase the interactivity of the page. Users can expand more content by clicking buttons. By browsing this content, users can have a clearer understanding of the product and decide whether this program is suitable for them. In addition, we also added the checked mark for the check box to ensure that color-blind people can also see that they have checked this box by the presentation of the checked mark, not only the color changing in this box. In the same way, we have increased the percentage under the progress bar to better serve the visually impaired.

Finally, for the card and drop-down box designs, we chose to keep the default designs because the client wants us to keep it simple. In addition, we believe that the color of the card is close to the background color of the page, which may not be conducive to people with visual impairments. Therefore, we recommend the client to consider using other colors to design cards.
